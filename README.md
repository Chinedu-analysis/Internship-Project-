# GlobalShala SuperheroU Ad Campaign Analysis

This project analyzes the performance of Facebook ad campaigns for GlobalShala's SuperheroU Program. The goal was to evaluate campaign effectiveness and provide actionable recommendations to optimize budget allocation and audience targeting.

---

## Project Overview

- **Objective:** Assess the performance of 11 Facebook ad campaigns across key metrics (reach, frequency, clicks, CPC, CPR) and identify underperforming campaigns.
- **Tools Used:** Power BI (dashboards, visualizations), Python (data processing, metric calculations).
- **Key Finding:** Campaigns targeting developed countries (USA, UK, Canada, Australia) significantly underperformed due to low Facebook engagement among teenagers in these regions.
- **Recommendation:** Discontinue underperforming campaigns and reallocate budget to platforms with higher teen engagement (Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat).

---

## Key Insights

- **Reach:** Campaigns 3, 4, 10, and 11 had reach far below the average (17,170).
- **Frequency:** These campaigns showed ads repeatedly to a small audience — a sign of poor targeting.
- **Cost Efficiency:** Campaigns 3 and 10 had high CPC and high CPR — the least efficient quadrant.
- **Audience Mismatch:** Facebook usage among teenagers in developed countries has dropped to 32% (Pew Research, 2022), explaining low campaign reach.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Discontinue Campaigns 3, 4, 10, and 11.**
2. **Reallocate resources to Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat** for developed markets.
3. **Maintain Facebook campaigns in developing regions** where teens still actively use the platform.

By discontinuing underperforming campaigns and shifting focus to platforms preferred by teenagers in developed markets, GlobalShala can significantly improve ROI and engagement for the SuperheroU Program.
